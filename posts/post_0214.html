Japanese ハンバーグ (Hamburg steak) vs. American Hamburger

Key Vocabulary & Phrases Learned

  • ハンバーグ (Hamburg steak)
  • ハンバーガー (Hamburger)
  • メキシコ料理 (Mexican cuisine)
  • フランス料理 (French cuisine)
  • 気温 (きおん) = Temperature
  • 生 (なま) = raw
  • すごい赤い肉 = very red meat
  • 気温が上がったり下がったりしています (The temperature is rising and falling)
  • どのくらいの頻度でハンバーグを食べますか？ (How often do you eat Hamburg steak?)
  • 週に一度食べます。 (I eat it once a week.)
  • 月曜日にしか食べられない。 (I can only eat it on Mondays.)

Main Discussion Points

Comparison between Japanese ハンバーグ and American Hamburger

  • Differences in preparation, texture, and serving style.
  • Japanese ハンバーグ is a soft, juicy patty often served with rice.
  • American Hamburger is usually in a bun with toppings.
  • Some restaurants in Japan serve ハンバーグ with a raw (生) center.

Cultural Differences

  • In America, hamburgers are more common and served with buns.
  • In Japan, ハンバーグ is eaten more like a meat dish with rice.
  • In China, bread and hamburgers are eaten together.
